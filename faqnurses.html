Nurse FAQ:

  • How long will the catheter remain in place?

    • The catheter will remain in place until fluid stops draining. Otherwise, the amount of time will vary from patient to patient, however the catheter could remain in place as long as needed. Fluid build-up may stop in the chest but it is unlikely to stop in the abdomen. 

  • When will the sutures (stiches) be removed?

    • Your nurse should remove these within 12-14 days.

  • What if patient is feeling short of breath or feeling discomfort after draining?

    • Slight discomfort after drainage will normally subside in a short period of time. However, if the patient continues to experience shortness of breath, and or pain after drainage consult the doctor.

  • What if the catheter accidentally gets pulled out?

    • In the unlikely event that the catheter gets pulled out, or the polyester cuff is exposed, you should cover the exit site with a sterile Tegaderm dressing as soon as possible, and seek immediate medical attention.

  • What should I do if the color of the fluid changes from the usual color?

    • Any change in color or appearance should be reported to your homecare nurse or doctor.

  • Can I take a shower?

    • You can take a shower or sponge bath only if the dressing covering the catheter is securely and completely attached to the skin all around the dressing edges. If the gauze becomes wet while showering or during sponge bath, remove the dressing, clean and dry the area, a new dressing will need to be applied as instructed.

  • Can I take a bath?

    • Never allow the dressing covering the catheter to soak under water in tub, bath, pool, etc.
